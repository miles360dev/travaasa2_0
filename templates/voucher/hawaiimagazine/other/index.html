ISLAND SLIPPER TO OPEN NEW STORE
AT ROYAL HAWAIIAN CENTER


Island Slipper, the last remaining original manufacturer of made-in-Hawai‘i footwear, is set to open The Island Slipper Store at Royal Hawaiian Center to the public on Friday, May 10. Their original store is located at Ward Warehouse.



The “Male’Ana” (Wedding) Collection made exclusively for the
Grand Opening of the Royal Hawaiian Center store.


Island Slipper’s new store at Royal Hawaiian Center is a step back in time. Before the hotels, beach homes were a privileged and special place. It was where families and friends from the mainland could gather and enjoy the beauty of Waikīkī. The design concept for the new store beautifully intertwines the feeling of those grand old beach houses.

Their new retail store is in a bigger space that enables Island Slipper to better serve its customers and showcase its storied brand. Woven throughout the store are elements that honor Island Slipper’s founding family and current ‘ohana with reminders of what makes Island Slipper special.




Island Slipper craftsmen at the original Kaka’ako factory, 1955.


“Our desire was to create a fun place to shop where our customers feel at home,” said John Carpenter, owner, Island Slipper. “For more than 60 years, Island Slipper has made sandals for all of Hawai‘i. There should be a deep connection between who you are and where you’re from, and we’ve always tried to remain true to the lifestyle and people of our islands.”



Parisian models admiring the Island Slipper Collection
at the Halekulani Hotel, 1958.


“We are pleased to welcome Island Slipper to their new location at the Center,” said Marleen ‘Ākau, general manager of Royal Hawaiian Center. “Their locally made slippers will be popular with our patrons seeking the perfect island-style complement to any casual outfit."
